The highly anticipated Nexus Pro X laptop is, without a doubt, a bizarre mix of absolute brilliance and baffling design choices. On one hand, the processing power is genuinely astonishing. It handles massive data science workflows, complex machine learning models, and heavy data visualizations with zero lag, which is absolutely incredible for a machine this thin. I was completely amazed by how quickly it compiled my scripts! However, the battery life is incredibly disappointing. After just three hours of intense coding, the device completely died, leaving me extremely frustrated and annoyed. Furthermore, the keyboard feels terribly cheap, and the trackpad is weirdly unresponsive at times. It is undeniably powerful, and I absolutely love the gorgeous display, but I am seriously questioning if the exorbitant price tag is justified when the basic hardware feels this flawed. I am keeping it for now, but I genuinely hope the company fixes these terrible hardware issues before the next release.
